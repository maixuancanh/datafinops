# DataFinOps Commercial Plan

## Ideal Customer Profile

Small and regional sportsbooks, prediction products, sports media services, and quantitative teams with meaningful TxLINE spend, multiple internal consumers, defined latency/coverage needs, and spreadsheet-heavy procurement. The customer can begin in read-only shadow mode and never enable execution.

## Buyer and Value

- **Economic buyer:** COO, CFO, VP Operations, or Head of Trading/Product Infrastructure.
- **Champion:** procurement lead, data platform lead, or trading operations manager.
- **Users:** procurement, product/trading operations, finance, signers, auditors, and administrators.
- **Value hypothesis:** reduce avoidable data spend without under-covering products, shorten renewal analysis, and create a defensible approval-to-entitlement evidence chain.

The product must separate potential savings from verified realized savings. Success-fee billing, if offered, uses only the contracted realized-savings methodology and excludes disputed or reversed entries.

## Packaging

| Plan | Mode | Included scope |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline | Live-read | Inventory, usage import, allocation, requirements, renewal calendar, audit |
| Optimize | Live-read | Deterministic scenarios, recommendations, policy, approvals, finance forecast |
| Governed Execute | Opt-in write | Unsigned proposals, client signing, observation, activation, entitlement verification |
| Enterprise | Configurable | SSO, custom policies, integrations, retention, dedicated support/capacity, private topology |

Pricing combines a platform fee based on workspaces and managed TxLINE spend. A separate contracted success fee may use verified realized savings. Network fees and TxLINE subscription charges remain direct customer/vendor costs.

## Design-Partner Program

1. Verify TxLINE rights, network/program/API semantics, security, wallet, and accounting methodology.
2. Build a read-only baseline and reconcile it to procurement records.
3. Import at least one representative planning period and resolve unallocated/missing data.
4. Run recommendations in shadow mode through one renewal cycle.
5. Review under-coverage risk and agree hard constraints, policy, approval graph, and savings baseline.
6. Enable devnet or deterministic signer flow; live-write remains a later optional gate.

## Sales Proof Package

- Verified read-only inventory and freshness evidence.
- Current versus optimized scenario with independent constraint verification.
- Infeasible example proving requirements are not silently relaxed.
- Policy, separation-of-duties, immutable approval, and invalidation evidence.
- Non-custodial transaction summary and secret-corpus report.
- Confirmation-to-entitlement verification timeline.
- Reproducible realized-savings example and reversal behavior.
- Architecture, security, runbooks, SLOs, and support model.

## Commercial Metrics

- Time from access/mappings to verified baseline.
- Percentage of spend allocated and requirements with named owners.
- Recommendation forecast, accepted proposal, verified activation, and realized savings separately.
- Under-coverage incidents prevented or caught during review.
- Renewal lead time, approval cycle time, activation success, entitlement mismatch, and reconciliation age.
- Platform uptime, optimizer p95, support load, gross margin, retention, workspace expansion, and write-mode adoption.

## 12-Month Roadmap

- **Quarter 1:** design partners, baseline, imports, allocation, requirements, shadow optimization.
- **Quarter 2:** scenario comparison, policy, approvals, audit exports, renewal calendar.
- **Quarter 3:** devnet/client signing, observation, activation, entitlement verification, first opt-in live-write tenant.
- **Quarter 4:** finance close, enterprise integrations, dedicated topology, additional TxLINE product semantics.

## Contract Boundaries

Contracts define customer access authority, TxLINE rights, permitted data, network/program, import quality, currencies/tax/credits, requirements ownership, approval/signer authority, live-write limits, retention, audit, SLO/support, incident notification, savings method, and liability. DataFinOps supplies analysis and governed tools, not tax, accounting, legal, investment, betting, or custody advice.

## Expansion Thesis

After TxLINE product-market proof, a vendor-neutral procurement layer may be evaluated. New vendors require isolated adapters, comparable semantic models, rights review, solver changes, independent golden/property evidence, and no weakening of governance or non-custody.
